## Core Philosophy

- **Quality over volume.** 4 posts/week beats daily mediocre content.
- **Writing-first.** No video needed. Lara built 300k followers with zero viral videos — all written content. Writing converts better on LinkedIn because it's expert-led.
- **The real fight isn't AI vs people** — it's people who use AI well vs people who use AI badly.
- **Give everything away.** Share your full blueprint. Those who would steal it were never going to be your client. Those who read it and want help will pay you.
- **Stories are never about you** — they're about your reader, told through your lens.

## BEFORE WRITING: The Intake Process

**IMPORTANT:** Before drafting any LinkedIn post, Claude MUST ask these questions. Do not skip this step.

### Question 1: What mode are we in?

| Mode | Description | When to use |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Recycle** | Repurpose existing content (YouTube video, IG post, podcast, blog) into a LinkedIn post | You've already created content elsewhere and want to extract value from it on LinkedIn |
| **Native** | Original LinkedIn-first content — written specifically for the platform | You have a story, insight, trend, or idea that hasn't been published elsewhere |

### Question 2: What type of post?

| Post Type | Best for | Framework | Photo needed? |
|-----------|----------|-----------|---------------|
| **Heartfelt / Milestone** | Personal wins, family moments, vulnerability + lesson | SLAY | YES — real photo essential |
| **Educational Blueprint** | Sharing your process, case study, step-by-step | PAS or SLAY | Optional — screenshots/diagrams help |
| **Trend Jack** | Riding breaking news or a trending topic through your lens | PAS | Optional |
| **Authority Jack** | Borrowing credibility from known figures | SLAY | YES — photo with the person |
| **First Client / Project Win** | Celebrating a milestone in your business journey | SLAY | YES — candid/real photo |
| **Behind the Scenes** | Showing the messy, real side of building something | SLAY | YES — raw/unpolished photo |

### Question 3: What's your raw material?

Ask the user: *"What's the story, insight, or content you want to turn into a post? This could be a win, a failure, something you learned this week, a trend you spotted, or a YouTube video you want to repurpose."*

### Question 4: Do you have a photo?

Ask the user: *"Do you have a real photo to go with this? (Selfie, event photo, candid moment, screenshot.) Storytelling and milestone posts perform significantly better with an authentic photo."*

If no photo: steer toward Educational Blueprint or Trend Jack post types, OR suggest what kind of photo to take.

## Content Modes

### Recycle Mode: Repurposing Content for LinkedIn

When converting YouTube/IG/podcast content to LinkedIn:

1. **Extract the single best insight** — don't summarise the whole video. Find the one moment, framework, or story that would stop a LinkedIn scroller.
2. **Rewrite completely for the platform** — LinkedIn is not a link dump. Never post "Check out my new video" with a link. Write a standalone post that delivers value without clicking anything.
3. **Use the SLAY framework** to restructure the insight as a story → lesson → action → reader question.
4. **Add a LinkedIn-native hook** — 8 words, specific outcome, not a YouTube title.
5. **The link goes in the comments** (if at all) — never in the post body. LinkedIn suppresses posts with external links.

**Recycle template:**
```
[8-word hook drawn from the single best insight]

[Rehook — specific number or outcome]

[2-3 line story setup: context for the insight]

[The lesson/framework — broken into scannable steps]

[Feel-good close — what this means for the reader]

[Call to engage — question, not a link]
```

## Post Type Deep-Dives

### Heartfelt / Viral Storytelling Posts

This is LinkedIn's superpower. The platform thrives on professional vulnerability, family pride, and achievement shared with humility. These posts consistently go viral when done right.

**The Art of the Humble Brag:**
- Share the achievement through the lens of gratitude, family, or the people who helped you — NOT self-congratulation
- The post is about THEM (your family, your team, your reader) not about you
- Vulnerability is the hook. The business lesson is the retention.

**Structure (SLAY):**
```
[Emotional hook — POV statement or time-stamped moment]
"POV: You became the first millionaire in your family."
"Last night, I retired my dad."
"3 months ago I had zero clients and no website."

[Vulnerable context — 2-3 lines of honest backstory]
"I never thought I'd get to write this post."
"I started building my AI business from my kitchen table."

[The journey — what happened, briefly]
Link your personal story to your business/skill.
Every story must connect back to what you do professionally.

[Feel-good close — under 20 words, warm, not salesy]
"Long nights were worth it."
"If I can do this, what's stopping you?"

[Call to engage — question]
"What's the moment that changed everything for you?"
```

**Photo rules for heartfelt posts:**
- MUST include a real, authentic photo (not stock, not AI-generated)
- Candid > posed. Real moments > polished shots.
- Photos of family, photos of you at events, behind-the-scenes of the grind
- The photo makes the story feel honest. Without it, the same words feel like AI slop.

**What makes heartfelt posts cringe vs. viral:**

| Cringe (LinkedIn Lunatics) | Viral (Lara's method) |
|---|---|
| "I'm so excited to announce..." | "POV: [emotional moment]" |
| Selfie with unrelated corporate achievement | Photo of the real moment (dad smiling, team celebrating) |
| Achievement → "congratulate me" | Achievement → "here's what it taught me" → "what about you?" |
| No lesson, just bragging | Vulnerability + business lesson + reader empowerment |
| Generic motivational filler | Specific details (numbers, dates, names) |

### Educational Blueprint Posts

Share your complete process — not surface-level tips.

**Use the Broad → Narrow → Niche structure:**
- **Broad hook:** Appeals to wide audience ("My one-person business is about to cross $200k/month")
- **Narrow problem:** Filters to your target ("With zero outbound, zero cold emails")
- **Niche breakdown:** Your specific, detailed method (step-by-step)

**Key rules:**
- Lead with "How I..." not "How to..." — personal experience > generic advice
- Include specific numbers, timeframes, and results
- Each step should be concise enough to implement immediately
- Give EVERYTHING away — the blueprint itself is the marketing

**Photo/media:** Screenshots of results, simple diagrams, or carousel-style images help but aren't essential. The writing carries these posts.

### Trend Jack Posts

Ride a breaking news story or trending topic through your niche lens.

**Key rules:**
- LinkedIn gets news ~1 week after Twitter — you can ride trends early
- Just state what happened + your expert take. That's it.
- You don't need to be first — you need to be clear and relevant to YOUR audience
- Even a dormant account can get 2,000+ likes with a well-timed trend jack

**Photo/media:** Screenshot of the news source or a relevant stat. Not required.

### Authority Jack Posts

Borrow credibility from known figures using the halo effect.

**Key rules:**
- Take photos with well-known people whenever you meet them
- Tell a story about what you learned from them or the context of the meeting
- Tag them ONLY if you know they'll engage — unengaged tags mark your post as spam
- Combine with education or storytelling for maximum impact

**Photo/media:** MUST have a photo with the person. Without it, authority jacking doesn't work.

## Find Your Unfair Advantage

Before writing anything, define two things:

### Monetisable Expertise
Knowledge that directly changes someone's outcome in a way they'll pay for.

- Lead with "How I..." not "How to..."
- Include specific numbers, timeframes, and results
- Example: "Here's my exact lead generation strategy in 2026" (not "Here's how to get leads on LinkedIn")

### Strategic Arbitrage
Taking what works (trends, news, cultural moments) and filtering it through your story or skill.

- LinkedIn gets news ~1 week after Twitter — ride trends early
- Filter trending topics through your niche expertise
- Use your personal story to connect broad topics to your specific offering

### AI Prompt for Strategy
```
Based on what you know about me, write me a complete list of:
1. My unfair advantages in the market
2. My monetisable expertise topics (knowledge that directly changes someone's outcome in a way they'll pay for)
3. My strategic arbitrage topics (trending themes I can filter through my own story/skill)

For each topic, give me 3 hooks (8 words max each).
```

## The 4-3-2-1 Content Mix

- **4** posts per week (Mon, Tue, Thu, Fri — or whatever rhythm works)
- **3** content pillars:
  - **TAM content** (Total Addressable Market) — broad topics that attract a wide audience to you
  - **Growth content** — niche-specific posts about your exact service/expertise
  - **Sales content** — posts that directly convert (case studies, social proof, offers)
- **2** frameworks (SLAY + PAS)
- **1** consistent schedule — same days, same rhythm, every week

**Suggested weekly split:**
- Mon: Educational Blueprint (TAM — broad topic)
- Tue: Heartfelt Story or Authority Jack (Growth — personal brand)
- Thu: Trend Jack or Educational Blueprint (TAM or Growth)
- Fri: Case Study or Win Post (Sales — social proof)

## Writing Frameworks

### SLAY Framework
The primary framework. Maps to the Attention → Retention → Conversion engine:

| Letter | Element | Purpose | What to write |
|--------|---------|---------|---------------|
| **S** | Story | Gets ATTENTION | Open with a specific moment, time-stamped event, or emotional hook |
| **L** | Lesson | Gets RETENTION | Pivot into "here's how/why" — the educational meat |
| **A** | Actionable advice | Gets CONVERSION | Direct, implementable steps (keep each to ~8 words) |
| **Y** | You | Points back to READER | End with a question or motivational close about THEM |

### PAS Framework
For more direct, problem-focused posts:

| Element | Purpose | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **Problem** | Name the pain | "Founders, your LinkedIn content sucks" |
| **Agitate** | Make it urgent | "It's costing you thousands of dollars daily" |
| **Solution** | Deliver the fix | "Here's how to write content that sells:" |

## The Writing Checklist

Every LinkedIn post must pass these checks:

### Hook (Lines 1-2)
- [ ] **8 words max** per line (mobile cutoff point)
- [ ] Specific outcome or number included
- [ ] Second line is a "rehook" — equally compelling, makes them click "see more"
- [ ] Lead with result/story, NOT a generic list title

**Good:** "Last night I signed my first ever client." / "I started building my AI business 3 months ago."
**Bad:** "Here's 10 ways to get leads on LinkedIn"

### Body
- [ ] Varied sentence lengths (1 line → space → 1 line → space → 2 lines → space)
- [ ] F-shape scannable — skimmable like a landing page
- [ ] Each step/lesson is concise and immediately applicable
- [ ] Reader can implement ONE thing and get results

### Close
- [ ] Feel-good quote or motivational line (under 20 words)
- [ ] End with a **call to engage** (question) not a call to action (link)
- [ ] People remember the ending — make it warm, not salesy

### Formatting
- [ ] Line breaks used generously — mobile-first platform
- [ ] No walls of text — "TikTokification of LinkedIn content"
- [ ] Post length: 800-1,200 words max for educational, shorter for heartfelt

## Photo & Media Guide

| Post Type | Photo Required? | What works | What doesn't |
|-----------|----------------|------------|--------------|
| **Heartfelt / Milestone** | YES — essential | Candid family moments, real event photos, behind-the-scenes | Stock photos, AI-generated images, posed corporate headshots |
| **Authority Jack** | YES — essential | Photo with the person, event context visible | No photo = no halo effect |
| **First Client / Win** | YES — strongly recommended | Screenshot of the win, candid celebration, workspace photo | Generic "celebration" stock image |
| **Behind the Scenes** | YES — strongly recommended | Raw, unpolished, real (messy desk, late night, whiteboard) | Overly curated "behind the scenes" |
| **Educational Blueprint** | Optional | Screenshots of results, simple diagrams, carousel images | Unnecessary decorative graphics |
| **Trend Jack** | Optional | Screenshot of the news source, relevant chart/stat | Not needed — the writing carries it |

**General photo rules:**
- Candid > posed. Always.
- Get into the habit of taking photos throughout your week — events, meetings, wins, even failures
- A real photo makes identical words feel authentic instead of AI-generated
- LinkedIn is a writing platform — you never NEED video. Photos + writing is the winning combo.

## Engagement Rules

- **First 30 minutes are critical.** LinkedIn ranks content by early engagement. Be present to reply to comments immediately after posting. Block this time in your calendar.
- **15 minutes daily engaging** — comment on others' posts, start conversations, respond to DMs
- **Don't schedule and forget** — if you schedule a post, block 30 mins after it goes live to engage
- **Comments > likes** — LinkedIn weights comments heavily in distribution
- **Reply to every comment** in the first hour — this signals to the algorithm that the post is generating conversation

## Story Mining Methods

When stuck for content ideas:

1. **Weekly self-interview:** What happened this week? Wins? New clients? Events? Lessons?
2. **Voice note collection:** Throughout the week, voice-note random thoughts to ChatGPT. At week's end, ask for a summary of themes.
3. **Call transcripts:** Record client calls (with permission). Problems you solve on calls = content topics.
4. **Twitter → LinkedIn pipeline:** Spot trending topics on X/Twitter, write your take for LinkedIn (it arrives ~1 week later on LinkedIn).
5. **Calendar review:** Look back at your week — meetings, events, milestones are all content fuel.
6. **Recycle your best content:** Your top YouTube video insight, your best IG caption, your most popular podcast moment — all can become standalone LinkedIn posts.

## Quick-Start: Writing a Post

When asked to write a LinkedIn post, follow this process:

1. **Run the intake** — Ask the 4 intake questions (mode, post type, raw material, photo). Do NOT skip.
2. **Choose framework:** SLAY for story-led posts, PAS for problem-led posts
3. **If recycling:** Extract the single best insight, rewrite completely for LinkedIn (no link dumps)
4. **Draft using the checklist** — 8-word hooks, rehook second line, varied spacing, specific numbers
5. **Apply Broad → Narrow → Niche** to the hook structure
6. **Check photo guidance** — does this post type need a photo? Advise the user.
7. **End with engagement prompt** — question, not a link
8. **Review against the writing checklist** before presenting

### Output Format
Present the finished post in a code block (for easy copy-paste), followed by:
- **Framework used:** SLAY or PAS
- **Post type:** Heartfelt / Educational / Trend Jack / Authority Jack / Win / Behind the Scenes
- **Content pillar:** TAM / Growth / Sales
- **Photo recommendation:** What photo to use (or "not required" for educational/trend posts)
- **Posting tip:** "Post when you can be present for 30 mins after to engage. Reply to every comment in the first hour."
