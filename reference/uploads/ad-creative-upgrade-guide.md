# Ad Creative Upgrade Guide

How to go from "stock photos the client gave me" to ads that actually convert. Based on Alfie's approach + Meta/Google best practices.

---

## The Problem With Basic Stock Images

Stock photos scream "ad" and people scroll past them. They're generic, they don't build trust, and they look like every other ad in the feed. Real photos outperform stock 2-3x on CTR — but only if they're done right.

---

## Alfie's Ad Creative Style (What Works)

Based on his best-performing ads ($1,642 → 230 leads):

### Visual Formula
```
REAL PHOTO (work being done / team / results)
+ TEXT OVERLAY (hook + key stat)
+ BRAND COLOUR (consistent across all ads)
```

### What His Ads Look Like
1. **Real photo** as the background — not stock, but actual work/results
2. **Bold text overlay** with the hook stat ("500+ Appointments Booked")
3. **Simple brand bar** at bottom with logo + CTA
4. **High contrast** — text readable on any background
5. **Square (1:1)** for feed, vertical (9:16) for Stories/Reels

### The 3 Ad Creative Types That Convert

| Type | When | Example |
|------|------|---------|
| **Proof/Results** | You have a case study | Photo of the work + "$X result in Y days" overlay |
| **Before/After** | Visual transformation | Side-by-side or carousel — ugly old site vs new site |
| **Behind the scenes** | Build trust + authority | You working, screen recording, on-site |

## How to Upgrade Your Ads in Canva

### Step 1: Get Better Source Images

**From the client:**
- Ask for 5-10 photos: team at work, finished jobs, equipment, happy customers
- Phone photos are fine — natural light, landscape orientation
- Tell them: "Just take a photo next time you're on a job"

**If client has nothing:**
- Screenshot their new website (before/after comparison)
- Use Google Street View of their business
- Take a photo yourself if you visit them
- Use their Google Business Profile photos

**Last resort (stock photos that don't look stock):**
- Unsplash.com or Pexels.com — search for real-looking trade/service photos
- Avoid: posed handshake photos, people pointing at screens, fake smiles
- Look for: candid work shots, real equipment, Australian-looking settings

### Step 3: Canva Pro Tips

1. **Dark overlay on photos:** Add a black rectangle over the photo → set opacity to 60-70% → put white text on top. Makes any photo work as an ad background.

2. **Consistent brand colours:** Pick 2 colours per client (primary + accent). Use for CTA bars, text highlights, borders. Save as a Canva Brand Kit.

3. **Font stack:** Use max 2 fonts per ad:
   - Headline: bold sans-serif (Montserrat, Poppins, or Inter)
   - Body/CTA: same font, regular weight
   - Never use script/decorative fonts

4. **Text hierarchy:** One big number/statement + one supporting line + one CTA. That's it. Don't cram copy into the image — that's what the ad text field is for.

5. **The 20% text rule:** Meta used to reject ads with >20% text on the image. They've relaxed this, but less text still performs better. Keep text overlay minimal.

6. **Save as PNG** (not JPG) for sharper text. Export at 2x for retina screens.

7. **Batch create:** Build one ad, then duplicate and swap photo/text for Ad B. Keeps visual consistency.

## How This Improves Your Paid Ads Skills

### Updates to make in your Meta Ads workflow:

**Phase 5 (Ad Creative) — add these rules:**
- Always create ads using Canva templates (not uploading raw photos)
- Dark overlay + white text on every image ad
- Include one specific number/stat in the image if possible
- Before/after screenshots are the #1 creative for website services
- Test: 1 "proof" ad + 1 "before/after" ad per ad set

**Phase 10 (Weekly Optimisation) — creative refresh cadence:**
- Swap underperforming creatives every 2 weeks
- Keep the same template structure, just change the photo and headline
- Screenshot new client websites as they go live — instant fresh creative

### Updates to make in your Google Ads workflow:

Google Search Ads don't use images (text only), but:
- **Image extensions** (Phase 8) — use the same Canva-quality images
- **Performance Max** (if you ever use it) — needs 5+ high-quality images. Use your Canva templates.
