# Follow-Up Emails

Different follow-up templates depending on where you are in the sales cycle.

---

## After Discovery Call (no response to quote)

**Subject:** Following up — [Business Name] website

**Body:**

Hi [Name],

Just checking in — I know things get busy. Did you get a chance to look over what we discussed for [Business Name]'s website?

Happy to answer any questions, or if the timing's not right, no worries at all. I'll be here when you're ready.

Cheers,
Aaron

---

## After Cold Outreach (no response)

**Subject:** Re: [original subject line]

**Body:**

Hi [Name],

Just following up on my note from last week. Totally understand if you're flat out — just didn't want it to get buried.

If you're interested in a quick chat about your website, I'm around this week. If not, no stress.

Cheers,
Aaron

---

## After Quote Sent (decision pending)

**Subject:** Any questions on the quote?

**Body:**

Hi [Name],

Just touching base — wanted to make sure the quote made sense and see if you had any questions.

If you're keen to go ahead, I can usually have things moving within a day or two of payment. And if you need to adjust anything, happy to chat.

Cheers,
Aaron

---

## "Revive" (client went cold 2-4 weeks ago)

**Subject:** Still thinking about the website?

**Body:**

Hi [Name],

I know we chatted a few weeks ago about [Business Name]'s website. Just wanted to check in — no pressure, just keeping the door open.

If anything's changed or you've got questions, I'm happy to pick up where we left off.

Cheers,
Aaron

---

## Notes for Aaron

- **Max 2 follow-ups** on cold outreach — after that, move on. Don't be that guy.
- **3-5 day gap** between follow-ups
- **After discovery call:** 1 follow-up at 3 days, 1 at 7 days, then stop
- **After quote:** 1 follow-up at 3 days, 1 at 7 days. If still no response, send the "Revive" at 3-4 weeks.
- **Tone:** Never guilt-trip, never "just checking in for the 4th time". Keep it genuinely casual.
- **Track:** Log follow-ups in CRM so you don't double-up
