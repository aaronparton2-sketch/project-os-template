# Setting Up Your Ads — Quick Access Guide

Hi! To get your ads up and running, I need access to a few of your accounts. This guide walks you through each one step by step. It should take about 10 minutes total.

If you get stuck on any step, just send me a screenshot of what you're seeing and I'll help.

## 1. Facebook Page Access

I need admin access to your Facebook Business Page so I can create and manage ads for you.

**On your phone (Facebook app):**

1. Open the **Facebook app** and go to your Business Page
2. Tap the **gear icon** (Settings) — usually top-right or under "More"
3. Tap **Page access** or **Page roles**
4. Tap **Add New** (under "People with Facebook access")
5. Search for my name: **Aaron Parton**
   - If it doesn't find me, use my email: **[your email]**
6. Set the role to **Admin** (this lets me manage ads — I won't change your posts or page info)
7. Tap **Give Access**
8. You may need to enter your Facebook password to confirm

**On a computer:**

1. Go to your Facebook Page
2. Click **Settings** (bottom-left of the page menu, or under "Professional dashboard")
3. Click **Page access** (left sidebar)
4. Under "People with Facebook access", click **Add New**
5. Search for **Aaron Parton** or enter my email
6. Select **Full control** (needed to create ad campaigns)
7. Click **Give Access** and confirm with your password

**What you should see:** A confirmation that access has been granted. I'll get a notification on my end.

## 2. Instagram Access

If you have an Instagram account for your business, I need it linked to your Facebook Page. This lets us run Instagram ads too.

**Step A — Make sure your Instagram is a Professional account:**

1. Open the **Instagram app**
2. Go to your profile → tap the **three lines** (top-right) → **Settings and privacy**
3. Scroll down to **Account type and tools**
4. If it says "Switch to professional account" — tap it and follow the prompts (choose **Business**, not Creator)
5. If it already says "Professional account" or "Business" — you're good, skip to Step B

**Step B — Link Instagram to your Facebook Page:**

1. In the **Instagram app**, go to your profile
2. Tap **Edit profile**
3. Tap **Page** (under "Profile information" or "Public business information")
4. Select your Facebook Business Page from the list
5. If your page doesn't appear, make sure you're logged into the same Facebook account that owns the page

**What you should see:** Your Facebook Page name should appear under your Instagram profile settings.

## 3. Google Business Profile Access

If you have a Google Business Profile (the listing that shows up on Google Maps), I need access to add location info to your Google Ads.

1. Go to **business.google.com** on a computer (or search "Google Business Profile" and sign in)
2. Click on your business name
3. Click the **three dots** menu (top-right) or go to **Settings**
4. Click **Managers** or **Users**
5. Click the **+ Add** button (or "Add users")
6. Enter my email: **[your email]**
7. Set the role to **Manager** (this lets me link it to Google Ads — I won't change your listing info)
8. Click **Invite** or **Add**

**What you should see:** A confirmation that the invitation was sent. I'll accept it on my end.

**Don't have a Google Business Profile?** No worries — I can set one up for you as part of the ad setup. Just let me know.

## 4. Payment Card for Ad Spend

The ad platforms (Google and Facebook) charge you directly for ad spend. I'll add your card during setup — you'll receive receipts directly from Google/Facebook.

**Option A — Tell me over the phone:**
I can add your card while we're on a call together.

**Option B — Add it yourself (Facebook):**
1. Go to **facebook.com/ads/manager/account_settings**
2. Click **Payment Settings**
3. Click **Add Payment Method**
4. Enter your card details
5. Let me know when done

**Option C — Add it yourself (Google):**
1. Go to **ads.google.com** and sign in
2. Click the **wrench icon** (Tools) → **Billing** → **Settings**
3. Click **Add payment method**
4. Enter your card details
5. Let me know when done

**Important:** The ad spend goes directly to Google/Facebook — not to me. My management fee is billed separately.

## 5. Photos and Videos

Real photos of your work, team, and equipment always perform better than stock images in ads. Even phone photos are great.

**What to send:**
- You and your team at work
- Finished jobs / completed projects
- Equipment or vehicles
- Happy customers (with their permission)
- Before and after shots
- Your logo (high-res if possible)

**How to send:** Email them to me, or share a Google Drive / Dropbox folder. Even texting them works.

**Don't have good photos yet?** I can start with what's on your website or Facebook Page and we can upgrade later.
