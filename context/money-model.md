## Revenue Goal

- **Target:** $10,000/month from website builds + growing monthly recurring revenue (MRR)
- **Stretch:** Scale to hire contractors (sales + AI devs) once MRR covers base costs
- **Lifestyle:** Flexible enough to work remotely (Bali, travel) while scaling

---

## Sales Philosophy (Hormozi-Inspired)

1. **Lead with a free offer** — remove all risk. The free website gets you in the door.
2. **Proof of concept close** — build it, show it. "If you don't like it, no charge."
3. **Upsell until they say no** — keep offering services on the call.
4. **Down-sell when they say no** — always have a zero-risk fallback so you never leave empty-handed.
5. **Become their "internet partner"** — stack MRR so deep they'd never want to leave.

## The Offer Ladder (Detailed Breakdowns)

Each tier below expands on the master table with full pricing rationale, AU market benchmarks, and close tactics.

### Tier 0 — Free Website (Decoy Offer)

| Item | Price | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| Basic skeleton website | **FREE** | Simple, functional site built from a template. 1-2 hours work. Delivered as promised — client gets the code. |
| GitHub repo access | Included | Full code access — they own it. Shared after the discovery call. |
| Monthly hosting | **$0** (or $29–$49/mo if they want us to host) | Default: send them the code, they're done. Optional: if they want us to host and maintain, basic monthly fee. |

**Purpose:** Get in the door. Build trust. Get a video testimonial. Create the contrast for Tier 1. This is the decoy — the real money is in what comes next.

**What they get:** A super basic, functional website. Not ugly, but not premium. Good enough for a basic online presence. No automations, no CRM, no SEO. Just a clean skeleton with their business info.

**Sales approach:**
1. Find business on Google Maps with no website (or a bad one)
2. Call/message: "I'll build you a free website — no strings"
3. Build a basic skeleton in 1-2 hours
4. Show it on a call → deliver it → send them the GitHub repo (code is theirs)
5. Get a video testimonial of the handover
6. Then: "This is great for a basic presence. But if you want something that actually brings in leads, let me show you what a professional version looks like..."
7. Show them the premium version side-by-side → pitch Tier 1

## Pricing Principles

1. **Lead with free.** The free website gets you in the door. The money is in the monthly stack behind it.
2. **Price on value, not hours.** Client pays for a working website + automation, not "4 hours of vibe coding."
3. **Always charge setup + monthly.** Setup covers your build time. Monthly creates the recurring flywheel.
4. **Never discount the monthly.** Discount the upfront if needed to close, but protect MRR.
5. **Upsell until they say no.** Every client interaction is a chance to offer the next tier.
6. **Down-sell when they say no.** Always have a zero-risk fallback — never leave a call empty-handed.
7. **Proof of concept removes objections.** Build it, show it, offer full refund. Risk reversal is the ultimate close.
8. **Review pricing quarterly.** As demand grows and you're closing >80% of leads, prices are too low.

```
    ↓

```
