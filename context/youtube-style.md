# YouTube Style Guide

> Claude reads this when generating video scripts, briefs, and content plans.
> This is Aaron's voice — every script should sound like him talking to a mate, not a corporate presenter.

---

## Voice & Tone

- **Energy:** Relaxed when talking to camera, fast-paced during B-roll and transitions. Energy spikes at jokes and reveals, then settles back to conversational.
- **Tone:** Casual, self-deprecating, educational. "Aussie bloke who happens to know about AI" — not "AI expert lecturing you."
- **Humor:** Deadpan delivery with unexpected pauses. Let jokes land — don't rush past them. Self-deprecating > sarcastic. Physical comedy and absurd visuals (AI images) over wordplay.
- **Language:** Natural Australian English. "Bloke", "mate", "stoked". No corporate jargon. If a technical term is used, immediately explain it in plain language (e.g., the API receptionist analogy).
- **Credibility signals:** Show the work (screen recordings, real device demos), not credentials. "I built this" > "I have X years experience."

## Recurring Motifs

- **Misty** — Aaron's pet dingo, referred to as "co-founder." Use for comedic B-roll (approving projects, sitting at desk, running on beach). She's the brand mascot.
- **Claude Code** — the AI tool. Always credited honestly — show what it did well AND what needed human iteration.
- **Vibe coding** — the overarching theme. AI-assisted development by someone without a traditional coding background. Aspirational for viewers.
- **WA lifestyle** — surf, ocean, outdoors. Not forced — it's genuine context for why Aaron builds what he builds.

## Structure Preferences

Every video follows this skeleton:

```
Hook          (0:00 - 0:15)   — 1-2 sentences max. Punchy. Sets the promise.
Why + Intro   (0:15 - 1:00)   — Why this matters. Brief channel intro. Keep under 60s.
Body 1        (1:00 - 2:00)   — The problem / context / setup
Body 2        (2:00 - 3:30)   — The build / process / how it works
Body 3        (3:30 - 4:30)   — The result / polish / what happened
Outro + CTA   (4:30 - 5:30)   — Wrap up. Two CTAs max: primary (value) + secondary (business).
```

- Body sections can expand to B4/B5 for longer videos (7-10 min)
- Each body section should have a mini-payoff — don't defer all satisfaction to the end
- Use "retention bridges" between sections: "Before I show you the result, here's where it got interesting..."

## Visual Style

- **A-roll:** Talking head, good lighting, clean background. Lots of hand gestures. Natural, not scripted-feeling.
- **B-roll:** Fast cuts. Screen recordings (VS Code, simulator, websites), side angles at desk, outdoor lifestyle shots.
- **AI images:** Clean illustration / cartoon style. Slightly cartoonish, professional but playful. Used for humor and to visualize abstract concepts (APIs, code processes). NOT photorealistic.
- **Screen recordings:** Sped up 2-4x with key moments at normal speed. Always annotated with zoom-ins on important UI elements.
- **Captions:** Always on. Most viewers watch with sound off first.

---

## Thumbnail Principles

- Aaron's face with exaggerated expression (surprised, stoked, excited)
- 3D render or clean screenshot of the product/build
- Tool/brand logo if relevant (Claude Code, product logo, etc.)
- Max 3-4 words of text overlay
- High contrast, bright colors — must read at 120px wide (phone thumbnail size)

---

## CTA Patterns

- **Primary CTA:** Always the immediate value for the viewer (download, free resource, link in description)
- **Secondary CTA:** Business pitch (Mycelium AI contact form) — keep it shorter, genuine, not salesy
- **Subscribe ask:** Weave naturally into outro, not as a separate "smash that subscribe button" moment
- **Comment prompt:** Optional. Use when there's a natural question to ask the audience.

---

## What NOT to Do

- Don't start with "Hey guys, welcome back to my channel"
- Don't apologize for video quality or being new
- Don't use clickbait that the video can't deliver on
- Don't spend >60 seconds on the intro before delivering value
- Don't use jargon without immediately explaining it
- Don't hard-sell Mycelium AI — let the work speak for itself
